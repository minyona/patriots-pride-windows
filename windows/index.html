Windows styles & materials

  • Single-Hung — A classic, budget-friendly profile — a fixed top sash with a movable bottom sash for easy vertical operation.
  • Double-Hung — Both sashes move independently and tilt in for cleaning — our most popular style for airflow and effortless maintenance.
  • Casement — Cranks open like a door for maximum ventilation, with a tight, energy-efficient seal when closed.
  • Picture — Fixed in place for a wide, unobstructed view that floods the room with natural light.
  • Bay — Three windows angled outward to extend your living space and add panoramic views and a built-in ledge.
  • Bow — Four or more windows in a gentle curve, creating a rounded, elegant projection from the wall.
  • Awning — Hinged at the top and opening outward, venting fresh air even in light rain — ideal for baths and basements.
  • Arched — A rounded top that adds a classic, sophisticated architectural accent to traditional and modern homes.
  • Palladian — A large central arched window flanked by two smaller rectangular windows — a dramatic focal point.
  • Custom — Geometric shapes, specialty sizes and one-off configurations built to fit your home's architecture.

All built on our Patriot 3000 and Patriot 7400 vinyl and composite series — engineered for superior insulation, reinforced frames and Low-E, energy-efficient glass that won't warp, fade or crack.

How to choose your windows

Start with what you're replacing. Old single-pane or worn early-vinyl windows are where you'll feel the biggest jump in comfort and energy savings; failing seals (fog between the panes) and drafts are the clearest signals it's time. From there it's three decisions: material — budget-friendly, low-maintenance vinyl versus stronger, paint-holding fiberglass; style — easy-cleaning double-hung, tight-sealing casement, single-hung, or a bay/bow to open up a room; and glass package — for the Tennessee Valley, ENERGY STAR Low-E with argon fill is the one that pays you back on summer cooling bills.

Because we're manufacturer-direct on seven window lines, we size the recommendation to your home and budget instead of pushing a single product — and we'll tell you honestly when your existing windows are worth keeping.
